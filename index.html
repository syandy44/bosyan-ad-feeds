Trend 01 · Anti-AI

The Anti-AI Wave

Etsy, Primark, Oscar Mayer — brands racing to own "human" before the space gets crowded. Warmth, imperfection, and handmade authenticity are becoming premium brand signals. Watch this arc run through Q3 and into holiday season.

Trend 02 · Sports

World Cup Land Grab

With the World Cup on U.S. soil, Nike, Visa, and dozens of others are staking out territory early. Playbook shifting from sponsorship spots to multi-week content arcs. Nike's 12-week cast reveal is the clearest signal yet.

Trend 03 · Craft

Simplicity Wins at Cannes

This year's Grand Prix haul skews toward simple, provable ideas. Heinz pointing at a box. A frog sound in a car. A stadium barcode. Clarity is still the ultimate creative flex — and the jury agreed unanimously.

Trend 04 · Purpose

Sustainability as Medium

Plenitude's dark mode ad tool treats the ad itself as a carbon problem. SKF's Faroe Islands program embeds a client into a renewable mission. Sustainability isn't the message — it's the mechanism.

→
Bottom Line

The industry is at an inflection point between AI-optimized efficiency and human-rooted resonance. The brands winning right now have picked a side — and have the creative nerve to commit fully. The fence is getting increasingly uncomfortable.

🏆 Cannes 2026 WinnerGUT São Paulo
Felipe Caro & Team GUT São Paulo
Creative Directors — Mercado Livre "Field Barcode"
"The brief was simple: make people scan. The insight was simpler: the stadium already is a barcode."
GUT's São Paulo office pulled off the outdoor stunt of the year — physically mowing a 104-metre barcode into Pacaembu Stadium's pitch. The work drove 53,000 coupon uses and 1.7M in sales, earning the Outdoor Grand Prix. What makes it brilliant isn't the scale — it's that the idea existed fully formed in a single sentence.
